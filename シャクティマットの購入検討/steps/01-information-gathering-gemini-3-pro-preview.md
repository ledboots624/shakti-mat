## 4. Scientific Evidence (科学的根拠)

**Overall Consensus:** **Medium Confidence.** Evidence supports benefits for pain and anxiety, but rigorous large-scale clinical trials are limited compared to pharmaceutical standards.

*   **Claim (Back Pain):** **Supported.** A 2021 randomized controlled trial found acupressure mats combined with exercise significantly reduced chronic low back pain compared to exercise alone.
    *   **Evidence:** Reduction in pain intensity and improvement in Quality of Life scores.
*   **Claim (Anxiety/Stress):** **Supported.** Meta-analyses indicate acupressure reduces anxiety. Physiological markers (heart rate variability, cortisol) often show a relaxation response after the initial pain subsides (parasympathetic activation).
*   **Claim (Sleep):** **Anecdotal/Partial Support.** While users overwhelmingly report better sleep, clinical evidence is less robust than for pain. The mechanism is likely secondary to pain relief and relaxation.
*   **Claim (Safety):** Generally safe. Adverse effects are minor (skin irritation, initial pain).
